📋 PSLE Hindi Paper Structure — Quick Reference

📝 Paper 1 — रचना (Writing)  50 min | 40 marks

  • Q1 — स्थिति रचना: Situational writing (scenario + bullet points, min 100 words)
  • Q2 — चित्र रचना: Picture story writing (6 pictures + word bank, min 100 words)
  • Answer only ONE question. 10 min to choose, 40 min to write.

📖 Paper 2 — Booklet A (MCQ)  35 questions | OAS

  • A1 (Q1–5): भाषा प्रयोग — Fill blanks in passage [5 marks]
  • A2 (Q6–10): भाषा प्रयोग — Fill blanks in passage [5 marks]
  • A3 (Q11–20): अर्थ ग्रहण — Cloze comprehension [20 marks]
  • A4 (Q21–27): आशय समझना — MCQ comprehension [14 marks]
  • A4 (Q28–32): शब्द ज्ञान — Synonyms [5 marks]
  • A5 (Q33–35): चित्र/नोटिस — Graphic comprehension [6 marks]

✍️ Paper 2 — Booklet B (Written)  40 marks

  • B6 (Q36–40): मुहावरे/लोकोक्तियाँ/सह-प्रयोग — Idioms & proverbs [10 marks]
  • B7 (Q41–45): वाक्य पूर्ति — Sentence completion (own words) [10 marks]
  • B8 (Q46–50): आशय समझना — Open-ended comprehension [20 marks]

🎧 Paper 3 — Listening Comp  Separate audio test

  • Listen to passages and answer MCQ/short-answer questions
  • Practice scripts available: Listening comp 2015–2020.pdf
  • Focus: main idea, specific details, speaker's attitude
  • Paper 4 — Oral: Reading aloud + Conversation

PHASE 1 — Foundation Building (Days 1–10)

Learn the exam format · Build vocabulary · Practise one section at a time · No time pressure · Use older papers (2012–2016)

Day 1
Easy
📚 Exam Orientation + Section A1 (भाषा प्रयोग)
Paper: 2014 — Paper 2 Booklet A, Q1–5  |  Section: A1 Language Use P2
0–5 minRead the Quick Reference card above. Understand all 4 papers and what each section tests.
5–30 minOpen 2014 Paper 2 Booklet A. Read the passage carefully. Fill Q1–5 blanks by choosing the best word/phrase from options. Focus on meaning in context.
30–40 minCheck answers using answer key (hindi-2012-2022_Answer_key.pdf). Understand why each answer is correct.
💡 Tip

For A1/A2 blanks, read the whole sentence (not just the blank). The tone and surrounding words will guide you.

Day 2
Easy
🔤 Vocabulary — शब्द ज्ञान (Synonyms)
Paper: 2015 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q28–32  |  Section: A4 Vocabulary P2
0–5 minReview 10 words from "Hindi PSLE vocab list 2022–2018.pdf" (first page). Say each word aloud.
5–30 minOpen 2015 Paper 2 Q28–32. For each word, try to think of the synonym BEFORE looking at options. Then choose the closest meaning from the 4 options.
30–40 minWrite a sentence using each of the 5 words you just practised. Check answers with key. Write down any new words.
💡 Tip

Synonyms = words with similar meanings. Common traps: words that look similar but mean something different (e.g., गर्व vs. गुरूर). Focus on EXACT match in meaning.

Day 3
Easy
📝 Language Use — A1 + A2 (भाषा प्रयोग)
Paper: 2013 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q1–10  |  Sections: A1 + A2 P2
0–5 minReview yesterday's new vocabulary words. Quick revision.
5–32 minOpen 2013 Paper 2 Q1–10. Two separate passages, each with 5 blanks. Read each passage fully first, then fill the blanks. Note: A1 is usually an informational/descriptive text; A2 is a personal/narrative text.
32–40 minCheck answers. For any wrong answer, read the sentence again and understand why the correct option fits better.
💡 Tip

A2 passages often test adjectives and adverbs that describe feelings or situations. Look for emotional cues in the text.

Day 4
Easy
📖 Cloze Comprehension (अर्थ ग्रहण) — First Attempt
Paper: 2012 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q11–20  |  Section: A3 Cloze P2
0–5 minRead the instructions for A3: 10 blanks in a continuous story passage, 20 marks. Each blank = 2 marks. MCQ options given.
5–30 minOpen 2012 Paper 2 Q11–20. Read the FULL passage first without looking at options. Understand the story. Then go back and fill each blank using context clues.
30–40 minCheck answers. Circle any blanks you got wrong. Re-read those sentences and understand the correct word choice.
💡 Tip

A3 is worth 20 marks — the most in Booklet A! Read the story like a reader, not just a test-taker. The story flow will naturally suggest the missing word.

Day 5
Easy
🔍 MCQ Comprehension (आशय समझना)
Paper: 2014 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q21–27  |  Section: A4 MCQ Comprehension P2
0–5 minUnderstand the 3 main question types: (a) Direct detail — answer is stated in the passage; (b) Inference — read between the lines; (c) Vocabulary in context — what does this word mean here?
5–32 minOpen 2014 Paper 2 Q21–27. Read the passage slowly. For each question, underline the relevant part of the passage. Choose the best answer from 4 options.
32–40 minCheck answers. For each wrong answer, find the exact line in the passage that proves the correct answer. This trains evidence-based reading.
💡 Tip

Eliminate obviously wrong answers first. Usually 2 options are clearly wrong; then decide between the remaining 2 by going back to the text.

Day 6
Easy
🖼️ Graphic / Notice Comprehension (चित्र/नोटिस आशय)
Paper: 2015 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q33–35  |  Section: A5 P2
0–5 minUnderstand Section A5: a notice, poster, or advertisement (चित्र/नोटिस) is given. 3 questions test specific details from it. Only 6 marks but easy to score full marks.
5–25 minOpen 2015 Paper 2 Q33–35. Carefully read every detail in the graphic — dates, times, conditions, eligibility, instructions. Answer Q33–35.
25–40 minAlso attempt 2016 Paper 2 Q33–35. Notices and posters often repeat similar structures. Check both sets of answers.
💡 Tip

Every word in a notice matters. Check for: कब (when), कहाँ (where), कौन (who can participate), क्या लाना है (what to bring). These are often the exact questions asked.

Day 7
Easy
✍️ Sentence Completion — वाक्य पूर्ति (B7)
Paper: 2013 Paper 2 Booklet B, Q41–45  |  Section: B7 P2
0–5 minUnderstand B7: Fill 5 sentence blanks with your own appropriate word. No options given. Must fit grammatically AND in meaning. 2 marks per question.
5–28 minOpen 2013 Paper 2 Q41–45. Read each sentence carefully. Think about: (a) what part of speech is needed (noun/verb/adjective)? (b) what meaning makes sense in context? Write your answer.
28–40 minCheck answers against model answers. Note: multiple correct words may be acceptable. Ask: is your word grammatically correct AND meaningful in context?
💡 Tip

B7 is open-ended — you need to supply the word yourself. Focus on words from the vocab list and idioms you've studied. Avoid repeating words from the same sentence.

Day 8
Easy
🗣️ Idioms & Proverbs — मुहावरे/लोकोक्तियाँ (B6)
Paper: 2014 Paper 2 Booklet B, Q36–40  |  Section: B6 P2
0–8 minUnderstand B6: A word bank of 10 words/phrases given. Fill 5 sentences with the correct idiom part. Sentences use common idioms (मुहावरे), proverbs (लोकोक्तियाँ), and word pairs (सह-प्रयोग).
8–30 minOpen 2014 Paper 2 Q36–40. Read each sentence and identify the idiom being referenced. Choose the correct word from the word bank. Learn the complete idiom for each answer.
30–40 minWrite out all 5 complete idioms/proverbs from today's practice. Memorise their meaning. Example: "नाक में दम करना" = to bother someone intensely.
💡 Tip

Common body-part idioms: हाथ, पाँव, आँख, नाक, कान, मुँह. Common proverbs use: आम, दूध, पानी. Recognise the idiom from keywords in the sentence.

Day 9
Easy
📝 Paper 1 — Situational Writing (स्थिति रचना)
Paper: 2015 Paper 1, Question 1  |  Section: Writing P1
0–5 minUnderstand Paper 1 Q1 structure: Given scenario + 4 bullet points. Must use ALL bullet points. Min 100 words. Scored on: content (covering all bullets), language (grammar, vocab), and expression (style, flow).
5–35 minOpen 2015 Paper 1 Q1. Plan briefly (2–3 min): what will you write for each bullet point? Then write your रचना — at least 100 words. Cover all 4 points naturally within the story/narrative.
35–40 minCount your words. Check: Did you use all 4 bullet points? Any spelling mistakes? Read aloud for flow. Aim to self-correct at least 3 things.
💡 Tip

Structure: Opening sentence (set the scene) → Cover each bullet point in order → Closing sentence (how you felt / what you learnt). Use time markers: पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, अंत में.

Day 10
Easy
🖼️ Paper 1 — Picture Story Writing (चित्र रचना)
Paper: 2013 Paper 1, Question 2  |  Section: Writing P1
0–5 minUnderstand Q2: 6 pictures tell a story. Picture 6 has a "?" — you create the ending. Given word bank (8–10 words) — use as many as you can. Min 100 words.
5–35 minOpen 2013 Paper 1 Q2. Look at pictures 1–5 carefully. Note the characters, actions, and setting. Plan your ending for picture 6. Write the full story using the given words and your own vocabulary. Be creative with the ending!
35–40 minCount words. Check: Did you use at least 5–6 words from the word bank? Is your ending logical based on the previous pictures? Self-correct grammar.
💡 Tip

For picture stories: describe what you SEE + add what characters THINK/FEEL + create DIALOGUE. This shows language richness. Use at least 2–3 dialogue sentences (said, replied, called out = कहा, बोला, पुकारा).

PHASE 2 — Skill Building (Days 11–20)

Combine sections · Work with longer passages · Attempt open-ended comprehension · Introduce time limits · Use 2017–2020 papers

Day 11
Medium
📝 A1 + A2 Combined (Language Use — 10 questions)
Paper: 2017 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q1–10  |  Timed: 18 minutes for both sections P2
0–5 minReview 10 new vocab words from vocab list. Also recall idioms learnt in Days 7–8.
5–23 minOpen 2017 Paper 2 Q1–10. Set a timer for 18 minutes. Complete both A1 and A2 under time pressure. Practice working efficiently.
23–40 minCheck answers. For every wrong answer, write the correct sentence in your notebook, explaining why that word fits. Build a personal error log.
💡 Tip

Real exam pace: 35 MCQ in about 60 minutes = ~1.7 min per question. A1+A2 (10 questions) should take ~17–18 minutes total.

Day 12
Medium
📖 Cloze Comprehension — Timed Practice (A3)
Paper: 2018 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q11–20  |  Timed: 20 minutes P2
0–5 minStrategy review: (1) Read full passage first; (2) Fill easy blanks first; (3) Use process of elimination for hard ones; (4) Check that the filled sentence flows naturally.
5–25 minOpen 2018 Paper 2 Q11–20. Set timer for 20 minutes. Attempt A3 (10 blanks, narrative passage). Focus on story coherence — each blank should make the story flow logically.
25–40 minCheck answers. Tally score out of 20. Identify which blank types you missed — vocabulary, grammar, or logic? Work on weakest area.
💡 Tip

A3 passages are often folk tales or stories with a moral. Blanks often test: (a) emotional words (was he happy? scared?), (b) verbs (what did he do next?), (c) connectors (then, therefore, however).

Day 13
Medium
🔍 MCQ + Vocabulary Combined (A4 full)
Paper: 2018 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q21–32  |  Timed: 20 minutes P2
0–3 minNote: Q21–27 = comprehension (7 questions, 14 marks); Q28–32 = synonyms (5 questions, 5 marks). Total 19 marks, 12 questions in 20 minutes.
3–23 minAttempt 2018 Paper 2 Q21–32 under timer. Read the comprehension passage carefully. Answer Q21–27 (comprehension), then Q28–32 (synonyms from the same passage text).
23–40 minCheck answers. For the 5 synonym questions — look at the word's context in the passage. Write each word's Hindi meaning in your notebook alongside 2 synonyms.
💡 Tip

Synonyms in Q28–32 are taken from the reading passage on page 7. If you're unsure of a synonym, look at how the word is used in the passage — the context helps narrow it down.

Day 14
Medium
🔤 Deep Vocabulary Drill — 2 Years' Synonyms
Papers: 2019 + 2020 Booklet A Q28–32 (both years)  |  + Vocab list review P2
0–5 minGo through your vocab notebook. Test yourself on all words you've written so far. How many can you recall without looking?
5–20 minDo 2019 Paper 2 Q28–32. Cover options, try to recall a synonym yourself first. Then check whether your answer matches one of the options.
20–35 minDo 2020 Paper 2 Q28–32 the same way. Note any words that appear in both years — these are frequently tested!
35–40 minAdd all new words to your vocab notebook. Organise by: emotions, actions, descriptions, nouns.
💡 Tip

High-frequency synonym words across years: लगाव (attachment), सहायता (help/support), गर्व (pride), उत्साह (enthusiasm), साहस (courage). Learn these cold!

Day 15
Medium
⏱️ Full Booklet A — Timed (35 MCQ)
Paper: 2016 Paper 2 Booklet A, Q1–35  |  Timed: 40 minutes straight P2
0–2 minSet timer for 38 minutes. Have pencil ready. This is a timed mock of the full Booklet A under exam conditions.
2–40 minComplete all 35 questions: A1(Q1–5) → A2(Q6–10) → A3(Q11–20) → A4 MCQ(Q21–27) → A4 Vocab(Q28–32) → A5(Q33–35). Do NOT stop during. Mark answers, move forward.
💡 Tip

Time allocation guide: A1+A2 = 15 min | A3 = 12 min | A4 MCQ = 8 min | A4 Vocab = 4 min | A5 = 4 min. If stuck on a question, mark it and come back — never leave blanks.

Day 16
Medium
💬 Idioms + Sentence Completion Combined (B6 + B7)
Paper: 2017 Paper 2 Booklet B, Q36–45  |  Sections: B6 + B7 P2
0–5 minQuick idiom review: recall all idioms you've learnt so far. Say each idiom + its meaning aloud.
5–20 minOpen 2017 Paper 2 Q36–40. Complete B6 (idioms/proverbs — word bank provided). Write complete idioms next to each answer.
20–35 minOpen 2017 Paper 2 Q41–45. Complete B7 (sentence completion — no options, your own words). Think about grammar and meaning carefully before writing.
35–40 minCheck both sections. For B7, score yourself: correct word (2 marks), partially correct/grammatically fitting (1 mark), wrong (0 marks).
💡 Tip

B7 common question patterns: जब... तब (when...then), ताकि (so that), क्योंकि (because), हालाँकि (although), इसलिए (therefore). These conjunctions signal what type of word fills the blank.

Day 17
Medium
📝 Open-Ended Comprehension — B8 (First Attempt)
Paper: 2018 Paper 2 Booklet B, Q46–50  |  Section: B8 P2
0–5 minUnderstand B8: Long passage (300–400 words). 5 open-ended questions worth 20 marks total (4 marks each). Answers must be in complete Hindi sentences. "2 विवरण दें" means give 2 specific details from the text.
5–10 minRead the 2018 Paper 2 B8 passage carefully. Underline key facts, names, dates, and reasons mentioned.
10–35 minAnswer Q46–50. Write in complete sentences. For "2 विवरण" questions, give exactly 2 separate points from the passage. Use your own words — don't just copy the passage.
35–40 minReview your answers against the model answers. Check: Did you answer what was asked? Did you include 2 points where required? Was your Hindi grammatically correct?
💡 Tip

B8 scoring: Each question = 4 marks (2 marks per detail/point). ALWAYS give 2 points if the question asks "2 विवरण दें". Never give 1 point and expect full marks.

Day 18
Medium
⏱️ Full Booklet B — Timed (Written sections)
Paper: 2019 Paper 2 Booklet B, Q36–50  |  Timed: 38 minutes P2
0–2 minSet timer. Plan your time: B6 (Q36–40) = 8 min | B7 (Q41–45) = 8 min | B8 passage reading = 5 min | B8 answers (Q46–50) = 17 min.
2–40 minComplete all of Booklet B under exam conditions. Write in full sentences where required. Keep moving — no dwelling on one question too long.
💡 Tip

In the real exam, Booklet B shares time with Booklet A (1h 40 min total). Booklet B should take ~55–60 min. Today's 38-min timed drill builds that stamina.

Day 19
Medium
✍️ Paper 1 — Writing Practice (Timed)
Paper: 2018 Paper 1  |  Choose Q1 or Q2 and complete in 35 minutes P1
0–5 minOpen 2018 Paper 1. Read both Q1 (situational) and Q2 (picture story). Choose the one you feel more confident with. Plan briefly: jot 3–4 key points you'll include.
5–40 minWrite your रचना in 35 minutes. Aim for 120–150 words (well above the 100-word minimum). Use varied vocabulary, time markers, and emotional descriptions. End with a strong concluding sentence.
💡 Tip

Marking criteria: (1) Content — covered all required points; (2) Language — correct grammar, varied vocabulary; (3) Expression — natural, engaging Hindi style. Aim for at least 2 in each category.

Day 20
Medium
🧩 Mini Mock — Mixed Sections from 2019
Paper: 2019 full paper — select key sections  |  Focus: Weakest areas identified so far P2
0–3 minLook back at your error log from Days 1–19. What sections did you score lowest in? Choose 2 weak sections to focus on today.
3–25 minAttempt your 2 weakest sections from 2019 Paper 2. Try to improve on your earlier score. Apply all strategies learnt so far.
25–35 minCheck answers. Compare with your earlier performance on similar sections. Are you improving?
35–40 minUpdate your vocab notebook with any new words from today. Write 3 new idioms if you haven't already.
💡 Tip

Phase 2 check-in: By now you should be scoring 70%+ on Booklet A MCQs. If any section is below 60%, revisit the Phase 1 day for that section before moving to Phase 3.

📚 Vocabulary Strategy — Build Your Personal Word Bank Daily

📓 Your Vocab Notebook

After each session, write any new words in 4 columns: Hindi word | Meaning | Synonym | Example sentence. Review this notebook every morning (5 minutes before study).

🔑 Key Vocab Files to Use

"Hindi PSLE vocab list 2022–2018.pdf" — use this as your core word bank. Cover meanings and test yourself daily. By Day 30, you should know 200+ words.

💬 Idiom Master List

Build a list of 30 idioms from all B6 sections practised. Each entry: idiom + meaning + example sentence. Review 5 idioms per day in Phase 3.

🎧 Listening Practice

Use "Listening comp 2015–2020.pdf" to read listening scripts aloud as a family. One person reads, the other answers. Builds comprehension AND speaking confidence for Paper 4 oral.
